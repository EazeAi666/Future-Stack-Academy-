## 📖 Description

### What is Future Stack Academy?

**Future Stack Academy** is not just a course platform — it is a creative movement.

Founded in **2026** by **Adesina Israel Ayomide (EazeAi)**, Future Stack Academy was built on a single, urgent observation: the creative industry is changing faster than most educational institutions can keep up with. Traditional design schools still teach the same fundamentals they taught a decade ago, while the professional landscape has been radically reshaped by artificial intelligence, generative tools, and an entirely new set of expectations from clients, employers, and audiences.

Future Stack Academy was created to close that gap.

We are an **independent online creative education platform** — structured with the rigour of an academic program and the agility of a modern tech product. We don't teach software for the sake of software. We don't teach AI for the sake of hype. We teach **design thinking, creative decision-making, and practical tool fluency** together, in a way that produces graduates who are genuinely ready to work, compete, and lead in the current creative economy.

## 🎯 What We Teach

Future Stack Academy delivers a full academic-grade curriculum across two core pillars: **Graphic Design Mastery** and **AI-Powered Creative Workflows**. Below is a complete breakdown of everything our program covers.

---

### 🎨 Pillar 1 — Graphic Design

#### 1. Design Fundamentals
The visual grammar behind every great design. Students learn the foundational rules and principles that govern all effective visual communication — the knowledge that never goes out of style regardless of which tools emerge next.

- **Color Theory** — hue, saturation, value, color psychology, accessible contrast, palette building
- **Composition & Layout** — rule of thirds, visual flow, balance, proximity, alignment, white space
- **Grid Systems** — column grids, modular grids, baseline grids, responsive grid thinking
- **Gestalt Principles** — how the human eye perceives visual relationships and groups
- **Visual Hierarchy** — directing attention, creating emphasis, guiding the viewer

#### 2. Typography Mastery
Type is the backbone of design. Our typography module goes far beyond just picking fonts — students learn to think and speak in type.

- Font anatomy and classification (serif, sans-serif, display, monospace, variable)
- Type pairing and contrast — making two fonts work together beautifully
- Hierarchy in editorial, UI, branding, and poster contexts
- OpenType features, kerning, tracking, leading — the details that separate amateurs from professionals
- Responsive and web typography
- Type as identity — how brands use custom type systems

#### 3. Brand Identity Design
Students learn to build complete brand systems from the ground up — the same process used at top design agencies worldwide.

- Logo design principles — symbol, wordmark, monogram, combination marks
- Building a visual language — shape vocabulary, illustration style, iconography
- Color systems and brand palettes
- Creating comprehensive brand style guides
- Multi-touchpoint rollout — business cards, packaging, social, signage, digital
- Presenting brand work to clients

#### 4. Print & Digital Production
Designing beautifully is only half the job — knowing how to prepare and output your work correctly is the other half.

- Print specifications — CMYK, bleed, trim, safe zones, resolution
- File formats and when to use each (PDF, EPS, AI, PNG, SVG, WebP)
- Designing for social media platforms — specs, ratios, safe zones
- Preparing assets for developers and printers
- Exporting and handoff workflows

#### 5. Poster, Editorial & Layout Design
Applied design thinking across real-world format types — posters, magazines, books, reports, presentations.

- Editorial layout — magazine and book spreads
- Event and promotional poster design
- Annual report and corporate document design
- Presentation design (pitch decks, keynotes)
- Designing for large format and environmental contexts
